My Care Companion · Beta Onboarding Guide

Beta Program

Welcome to the My Care Companion beta.

This short guide walks you through getting the beta installed on your iPhone, creating your account, and submitting your first piece of feedback. Read it once — should take about five minutes.

1 · What "beta" means

Beta is a working preview of My Care Companion released to a small group of users before the public launch. A few things to know:

  • Things may change without warning. A button might move; a screen might be redesigned. We're improving things based on what we hear from you.
  • You may see the occasional bug. If you do, please report it (instructions in section 5). Bug reports from beta users are how we ship a more polished product.
  • Your data is safe. The beta uses the same secure backend as the production app. Anything you log here is real, encrypted, and yours.
  • You can leave at any time by removing the app from TestFlight (instructions at the end).

2 · Installing TestFlight (iPhone)

Apple distributes all beta apps through a free app called TestFlight. It's safe, official, and the only way to get pre-release apps onto your iPhone.

Step 2a — install TestFlight if you don't already have it

  1. On your iPhone, open the App Store.
  2. Search for TestFlight.
  3. Tap Get and authenticate (Face ID / Touch ID / password).
[Screenshot: App Store search result for "TestFlight"] Shows the official Apple TestFlight icon (blue, white triangle propeller) and a Get button.

Step 2b — accept your invitation

Once you submitted your Apple ID on the onboarding page, Apple sent (or will shortly send) you an email titled something like "Bryan Stofle invited you to test My Care Companion." If you don't see it, check your spam folder.

  1. Open that email on your iPhone.
  2. Tap View in TestFlight.
  3. TestFlight will open. Tap Accept next to My Care Companion.
  4. Tap Install. The beta will appear on your home screen.
[Screenshot: TestFlight invitation screen showing "My Care Companion" with Accept/Install] Shows the app icon, version number, and a brief description of what's new.
Tip: The beta app icon will have a small orange dot beside it on your home screen. That's how you (and Apple) tell it apart from a normal App Store version.

3 · Creating your account

You can create your account on the iPhone or in a desktop browser. The data syncs automatically — pick whichever feels easier right now.

On the iPhone

  1. Open the My Care Companion app from your home screen.
  2. Tap Sign Up.
  3. Enter your name, email, and a password.
  4. You'll be asked to set up multi-factor authentication (MFA). Choose either a code sent by email or an authenticator app — both work fine.
  5. You'll land on the dashboard.
[Screenshot: Mobile app sign-up screen] Shows the email/password form, the "Sign Up" button, and the company logo.

On the web

Open https://dev.mycarecompanion.com in your browser, click Sign Up, and follow the same steps. You can use either device interchangeably.

[Screenshot: Web sign-up page] Same fields, larger layout. Includes the same MFA step.

4 · Adding your first child

My Care Companion is built around tracking one or more children. After you sign up:

  1. From the dashboard, tap or click Add a child.
  2. Enter their name, date of birth, and any conditions or diagnoses you want the app to be aware of.
  3. Save. You'll be returned to the dashboard, now with that child visible.
[Screenshot: Add Child form] Name, DOB, conditions multi-select. Big "Save" button at the bottom.

You can add medications, log behaviors, sleep, meals — whatever you want to track — from the child's dashboard. Take your time exploring; the app is designed so you don't have to log everything to get value from logging something.

5 · Filing a bug report (this is the most important section)

If something looks wrong, doesn't work the way you expected, or crashes — please report it. Even small things help. Bug reports from beta users are the single most valuable thing you can give us during this phase.

  1. From any screen, tap or click Support in the menu.
  2. Tap the + New Ticket button.
  3. In the Type dropdown, choose Bug Report.
  4. Fill in the subject and a description of what happened.
  5. Use the Attachments area to add screenshots or a short video. The beta supports both file upload and (on desktop) screen + microphone recording — that last one is incredibly useful: it lets you talk through what you're seeing while we watch the same thing.
  6. Submit.
[Screenshot: Support page with "+ New Ticket" form open, type dropdown showing Bug Report selected] Shows the priority selector, attachment widget with a 📎 button, and (on desktop) the 🎬 Record screen + mic button.
Heads up about privacy: Screenshots and screen recordings may capture personal health information. Our support team is allowed to see this material to help you, but every attachment is permanently deleted from our servers the moment we close your ticket. You can read more about this in the consent dialog the first time you attach something.

What "good" looks like

  • What you were doing when you noticed the problem ("I was trying to log a sleep entry…")
  • What you expected to happen ("…I expected the form to save.")
  • What actually happened ("…the page froze and I had to refresh.")
  • A screenshot or 30-second screen recording showing it.

6 · Submitting a feature request

If there's something you wish the app did, file it the same way:

  1. Support → + New Ticket.
  2. Type: Feature Request.
  3. Describe what you'd like to be able to do, and why.

Genuinely good ideas get added to our public product roadmap, and you'll get a notification when the feature ships — first to dev, then to production.

[Screenshot: New ticket form with Type dropdown showing Feature Request highlighted] Same form as a bug report, just a different type. The Attachments area still works if you want to mock up your idea.

7 · Where to get help

If you get stuck on any of this, you have three ways to reach us:

  • Send a Support ticket from the app (any type).
  • Reply to whoever invited you — they'll route the message to the right person.
  • Email support@mycarecompanion.net.

Leaving the beta

If you ever want to stop receiving the beta:

  1. Open the TestFlight app on your iPhone.
  2. Tap My Care Companion.
  3. Scroll to the bottom and tap Stop Testing.

Your account, data, and family stay intact — you'll just stop receiving pre-release builds. When the app launches publicly you can re-install from the regular App Store and pick right back up.

Thank you for being part of this — beta users are the reason My Care Companion gets to be a thoughtful, careful product instead of a generic one.
