CareCompanion / Dashboard Redesign · Round 2

Softer, simpler, less clinical.

Round 2 takes the tester's feedback and synthesizes it: keep Calm's floating quick-log button, keep Garden's encouraging bottom reminder, give every page a positive lead-in (with a fallback when there's nothing to highlight yet), and add a "View Matty's Full Day" drill-in plus an escape hatch to the full caregiver view. Four takes on the same vision, varying layout and voice.

All four use real Matty Steinmetz data. Round 1 (Calm Today, Garden, Story, Postcard, plus the rejected Bento and Clinical) is preserved verbatim at /static/mockups_v1/.

Direction 3 letter.html

Quiet Letter

The app writes a short, soft note about Matty's day. Inline chips let the parent act without leaving the prose.

  • Strongest "this is for me, not a chart" feel
  • Empty insights become a sentence, not an empty box
  • Lowest visual density — least overwhelming
  • Riskiest if the auto-templated prose ever reads stilted
  • More words to read than skim — slower to glance

Daily Headline

What every Round 2 mockup keeps

  • · Floating quick-log compose button (carried from Calm)
  • · Positive lead-in headline at top, with empty-state-aware fallback voice when no patterns/insights exist yet
  • · Garden-style "options" — 4 plant tiles + 6 seed buttons — either inline-expandable or always visible, in each mockup's softened palette
  • · "You don't have to log everything. Even one note helps us notice patterns." reminder near the bottom
  • · "Need every detail? Open the full caregiver view →" escape hatch to the existing dashboard for power users
