1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide

We collect information you voluntarily provide when using our Service, including:

  • Account Information: Name, email address, password, and contact information when you register for an account.
  • Family Information: Names, dates of birth, and relationships of family members you add to your account.
  • Child Health Information: Health logs, medication records, behavioral observations, sleep patterns, dietary information, and other care-related data you choose to enter about children in your care.
  • Medical Provider Information: Contact information for healthcare providers you choose to connect with your account.
  • Communications: Messages sent through our family chat feature and support requests.

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you access our Service, we may automatically collect:

  • Device information (device type, operating system, browser type)
  • Log data (access times, pages viewed, IP address)
  • Usage patterns and feature interactions

1.3 Mobile App Data

When you use our iOS or Android mobile application, we may additionally collect:

  • Push Notification Tokens: A device identifier used to send you push notifications (medication reminders, alerts, messages). This token is stored on our servers and associated with your user account. It does not contain personal information and cannot be used to track you across other apps.
  • Device Platform: Whether you are using iOS or Android, to deliver notifications correctly.
  • Device Name: An optional device label (e.g., "iPhone") to help you manage your registered devices.

Our iOS app sends push notifications via Apple Push Notification service (APNs) directly — no Firebase SDK is bundled in the iOS binary. Our Android app uses Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM), a service provided by Google, as a push-delivery transport. No health data is sent to Apple or Google. Only your device token and notification content (title, message text, and a link identifier) are transmitted through these push services. Neither Apple nor Google has access to your health records, care logs, or any child information.

4.1 AI-Assisted Pattern Analysis (AWS Bedrock under BAA)

To generate insights and identify patterns in your family's logged data, we use Anthropic's Claude AI models hosted on Amazon Web Services (AWS) Bedrock. AWS is our Service Provider for this processing and operates under a signed Business Associate Agreement (BAA), which contractually binds AWS to handle any health information in compliance with HIPAA standards. Anthropic does not receive or retain your inputs — Claude on Bedrock runs entirely within AWS infrastructure, and AWS does not use your data to train AI models.

What we send to the AI:

  • By default: de-identified data only — your child's age band, broad diagnosis categories, medication classes (e.g., "stimulant" rather than the brand name), and numeric daily logs (sleep duration, behavior counts, mood scores, etc.). We strip first/last names, exact dates of birth, and free-text notes before the data leaves our servers.
  • If you opt in (via Settings → AI Insights → "Allow AI to read my notes"): we additionally include your free-text notes and observations so the AI can interpret context that numbers alone cannot convey. This is off by default and can be turned off at any time from the same Settings panel. Opting in does not retroactively send historical notes — only new analyses run after you enable it.

The insights generated are stored in your account on our servers and shown only to you and the family members you have invited. We do not share AI-generated insights with anyone outside your family or care team. The Claude AI does not have access to other families' data — each request is scoped to a single child's profile and recent logs.

While we strive to protect your personal information, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security but commit to promptly notifying you of any breach affecting your data as required by Texas law.
